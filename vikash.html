Excursions

Discover the rich cultural and historical heritage around Kananwas Resort with our curated excursions. Explore magnificent forts, ancient temples, and breathtaking natural landscapes that offer a glimpse into the past and the beauty of Rajasthan.

Kumbhalgarh Fort

Kumbhalgarh fort is a Mewar fortress on the westerly range of Aravalli Hills. It is a World Heritage Site included in Hill Forts of Rajasthan. Built during the course of the 15th century by Rana Kumbha. Occupied until the late 19th century, the fort is now open to the public and is spectacularly lit for a few minutes each evening. With the walls of the fort extending over 38 km, it is claimed to be the second-longest continuous wall after the Great Wall of China the fort is among the largest wall complex in the world, and the second largest fort in India after Chittoor Fort. There are over 360 temples within the fort, 300 ancient Jain and the rest Hindu. From the palace top, it is possible to see kilometres into the Aravalli Range.

Ranakpur Temple

The Ranakpur temple is one of the largest and most important temples of Jain culture. The temple is located 50 kms from Kumbhalgarh. The construction of the temple started in the 15th century following a divine vision, Rana Kumbha supported the construction of the temple. The temple, with its distinctive domes, shikhara, turrets and cupolas rises majestically from the slope of a hill. 1444 marble pillars, carved in exquisite detail, support the temple. The pillars are all differently carved and no two pillars are the same. It is also said that it is impossible to count the pillars. Also all the statues face one or the other statue. There is one beautiful carving made out of a single marble rock where there 1008 heads of snakes and numerous tails. One cannot find the end of the tails.

Haldi Ghati

This mountain pass is a significant historical location. It is the site of the Battle of Haldighati, which took place in 1576 between the Kingdom of Mewar and the Mughal Army led by king Man Singh. The museum there depicts life of Maharana Pratap.

Jungle Safari

Embark on this exciting jeep safari tour in Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary of 2 hours and feel the thrill and excitement. Start your trip after getting picked up from the entry gate of Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary. Set off towards the sanctuary for an exciting jeep safari tour and witness the diversified wildlife. Witness some of the endangered species like wolf, leopards, sloth bear, hyena, jackal, jungle cat, sambhar and nilgai. Enjoy the gratifying bird life in the the wildlife sanctuary. End your tour by admiring the stunning landscapes and spotting some migratory birds. Entry is allowed from 6am to 8:30 am and 3pm to 5:30pm.
